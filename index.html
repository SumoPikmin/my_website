Projects

    Bachelor's Thesis: Evaluating Cultural Diversity in Text-to-Image Models
    (June 2024 – December 2024)

    During my Bachelor thesis, I developed a large-scale benchmark to evaluate cultural biases in cutting-edge Text-to-Image models such as Stable Diffusion XL and FLUX.1. By leveraging a multilingual dataset spanning 2500 concepts in five languages and over 2.3 million compositional prompts, I designed an innovative evaluation pipeline using advanced Visual Question Answering models like Qwen2-VL-7B-Instruct to accurately measure text-image alignment. This research revealed a significant performance gap of over 10% when generating content related to non-Western cultures, highlighting systemic cultural and multilingual biases in current generative AI systems. The project was awarded an outstanding grade of 1.0 (A+).
    Traffic Management System: Incident Module
    (Oct 2023 – March 2024)

    As part of a university software project at the Institute for Railway Systems, TU Darmstadt, I created a simulation and GUI-based Incident Management Module for the Train Management System using Python and Tkinter. This tool allowed interactive configuration and management of over 25 railway incident scenarios, supporting training and operational planning. I built a real-time simulation environment featuring incident visualization and implemented backend logic to execute scenarios and analyze system responses. By applying agile methodologies including Scrum and conducting thorough usability testing, the module achieved a 30% improvement in task efficiency, demonstrating both technical and practical impact.
